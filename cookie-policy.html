2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Similar technologies, such as local storage, pixels, and web beacons, may also store or read information from your browser. These technologies are commonly used to make websites work, remember preferences, understand performance, and improve user experience.

Cookies can be:

  • Session cookies: temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies: cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them.
  • First-party cookies: cookies set by the website you are visiting.
  • Third-party cookies: cookies set by a domain other than the website you are visiting.
